여우와 두루미

The Fox and the Crane

1. 여우의 장난

1. The Fox's Mischief

어느 숲속 마을에 여우와 두루미가 이웃하여 살고 있었다. 둘은 가끔 인사를 나누는 사이였으나, 깊이 어울린 적은 없었다. 어느 날, 여우가 두루미를 찾아왔다. 여우는 능글맞은 미소를 지으며 말했다.

"두루미 양, 내일 저녁에 우리 집으로 식사하러 오지 않겠소? 내가 특별한 요리를 준비하겠소."

"어머, 정말요? 고맙습니다, 여우 씨. 꼭 가겠어요."

두루미는 기뻐하며 돌아갔다. 그러나 여우는 속으로 킥킥거리며 혼자 웃었다.

'두루미가 저 길다란 부리로 어떻게 먹을지 구경 좀 해 볼까. 재미있겠는걸.'

여우는 처음부터 두루미를 놀려 먹을 속셈이었던 것이다.

  • 능글맞다: [adj.] Crafty and scheming on the inside while wearing a perfectly innocent face on the outside.
  • 킥킥거리다: [verb] To chuckle repeatedly while trying to stifle the sound. Onomatopoeia.
  • 놀리다: [verb] To tease someone mischievously or make them an object of laughter.
  • 속셈: [noun] A hidden calculation or plan kept privately in one's mind.
  • 두루미: [noun] A large bird of the crane family.

다음 날 저녁, 두루미는 설레는 마음으로 여우의 집을 찾아갔다. 여우의 집 안에는 고소한 수프 냄새가 가득했다. 두루미의 입안에 침이 고였다.

"어서 오시오, 두루미 양. 오늘은 내가 정성껏 끓인 수프를 대접하겠소."

여우는 수프를 넓적하고 얕은 접시에 담아 내놓았다. 여우는 넓은 혀로 접시를 핥아 수프를 맛있게 먹기 시작했다. 한 입, 두 입, 금세 반을 비웠다.

  • 설레다: [verb] For one's heart to flutter and race with excitement or anticipation.
  • 고소하다: [adj.] Having the rich, nutty taste or smell of roasted sesame or sesame oil.
  • 정성껏: [adv.] With all the care and devotion one can give; wholeheartedly.
  • 대접하다: [verb] To prepare food and serve it to a guest; to treat someone to a meal.
  • 넓적하다: [adj.] Flat and thin, yet fairly wide.

두루미는 긴 부리를 접시 위에 대 보았으나, 부리 끝만 수프에 닿을 뿐 한 모금도 제대로 떠먹을 수 없었다. 부리로 아무리 쪼아 보아도, 넓고 얕은 접시의 수프는 흘러내릴 뿐이었다.

"왜 안 드시오? 입맛에 안 맞소?"

여우는 걱정하는 척했으나, 입꼬리가 실룩실룩 올라가는 것을 감추지 못했다.

"아니에요, 맛있어요... 다만 제가 조금 배가 아파서요."

두루미는 창피함을 꾹 참으며 대답했다. 결국 두루미는 수프 한 모금 제대로 먹지 못한 채 빈속으로 돌아가야 했다. 여우는 두루미가 돌아간 뒤 혼자 남은 수프까지 깨끗이 핥아 먹으며 배를 잡고 웃었다.

'아이고, 배야! 두루미 꼴 좀 봐라. 저 긴 부리로 쩔쩔매는 게 얼마나 우습던지!'

  • 실룩실룩: [adv.] Describing the way a muscle or part of the face repeatedly quivers and twists to one side. Mimetic word.
  • 감추다: [verb] To cover or hide something so that others cannot see or find it.
  • 창피하다: [adj.] To feel mortified, as if one's dignity or face has been damaged.
  • 쩔쩔매다: [verb] To be so flustered that one cannot gather one's wits and fumbles about helplessly.
  • -던지: [ending] A connective ending showing that a remembered fact serves as the basis for the following surprise or exclamation.

1. The Fox's Mischief

In a village deep in the forest, a fox and a crane lived side by side as neighbors. The two would exchange greetings now and then, but they had never really grown close. Then one day, the fox paid the crane a visit. A crafty little smile played on his lips as he spoke.

- Fox: "Lady Crane, would you not come to my house for dinner tomorrow evening? I shall prepare a very special dish for you."

- Crane: "Oh my, truly? Thank you so kindly, Master Fox. I shall be sure to come."

Delighted, the crane made her way home. But the fox, left alone, chuckled softly to himself.

- Fox: 'Let's see how the crane manages to eat a single bite with that long beak of hers. This ought to be amusing.'

From the very beginning, the fox had been scheming to make a fool of the crane.

The next evening, the crane set off for the fox's house, her heart fluttering with anticipation. Inside his home, the rich, nutty aroma of soup filled every corner. The crane's mouth began to water.

- Fox: "Welcome, Lady Crane. Tonight I shall serve you a soup I have simmered with the greatest care."

The fox ladled the soup into a wide, shallow dish and set it out. Then, with his broad tongue, he began to lap at the dish, devouring the soup with relish. One lick, two licks, and in no time half the dish was gone.

The crane lowered her long beak to the dish, but only the very tip of it could touch the soup; she could not gather a single proper sip. No matter how she pecked, the soup in the wide, shallow dish only slipped away from her.

- Fox: "Why, you have not touched a thing. Is it not to your taste?"

The fox feigned concern, but he could not hide the corners of his mouth that kept quivering upward.

- Crane: "No, no, it is delicious... it is only that my stomach is a little out of sorts."

Swallowing her mortification, the crane answered as politely as she could. In the end, without a single proper sip of soup, she had to head home on an empty stomach. Once she was gone, the fox licked up every last drop of the leftover soup himself, then clutched his belly and roared with laughter.

- Fox: 'Oh, my aching sides! What a sight that crane was! How wonderfully ridiculous she looked, fumbling about with that long beak of hers!'

2. 두루미의 보답

2. The Crane's Repayment

며칠이 지났다. 두루미가 여우를 찾아왔다. 두루미는 부드러운 미소를 지으며 말했다.

"여우 씨, 지난번 식사 정말 감사했어요. 이번에는 제가 보답하고 싶어요. 내일 저녁에 우리 집으로 오시겠어요?"

"오, 그거 좋지! 두루미 양의 요리 솜씨를 맛볼 수 있다니 기대되는걸."

여우는 이번에도 맛있는 음식을 공짜로 먹을 수 있으리라 생각하며 흡족해했다.

'지난번에 저렇게 당하고도 나를 초대하다니. 역시 두루미는 순진하단 말이야.'

  • 보답: [noun] Repayment made in return for another's kindness or favor.
  • 솜씨: [noun] The skill or knack one has with one's hands for making or handling something.
  • 흡족하다: [adj.] Well-pleased; feeling fully satisfied with nothing lacking.
  • 당하다: [verb] To suffer or be subjected to something unpleasant or harmful.
  • -다니: [ending] A sentence-final ending expressing surprise or exclamation at something unexpected.

다음 날 저녁, 여우는 콧노래를 흥얼거리며 두루미의 집에 도착했다. 집 안에서 향긋한 생선 요리 냄새가 풍겨 나왔다. 여우는 침을 꿀꺽 삼켰다.

"어서 오세요, 여우 씨. 오늘은 제가 정성껏 만든 요리를 준비했답니다."

두루미가 내놓은 그릇을 보고 여우는 눈이 휘둥그레졌다. 요리는 목이 길고 입구가 좁은 병에 담겨 있었다. 두루미는 긴 부리를 병 속에 쑥 넣어 요리를 맛있게 집어 먹었다. 여우는 주둥이를 병 입구에 들이밀었으나, 입구가 너무 좁아서 코조차 들어가지 않았다. 병 기울여도 보고, 혀를 길게 내밀어도 보았으나, 혀끝에 닿을까 말까였다.

  • 흥얼거리다: [verb] To hum a tune repeatedly under one's breath, carried away by one's mood.
  • 향긋하다: [adj.] Having a subtly fragrant, pleasant scent.
  • 풍기다: [verb] (Of a smell) to drift out and spread; to give off a smell.
  • 휘둥그레지다: [verb] For one's eyes to pop wide open in surprise or fear.
  • 들이밀다: [verb] To shove or push something forcefully inward.

"왜 안 드세요? 혹시 입맛에 안 맞나요?"

두루미는 걱정하는 척 물었다. 그 말투는 지난번 여우의 말투와 꼭 닮아 있었다.

여우는 얼굴이 벌겋게 달아올랐다. 자기가 지난번에 두루미에게 했던 짓이 그대로 되돌아온 것이다. 여우는 아무 말도 하지 못하고 빈속으로 집에 돌아가야 했다. 텅 빈 배를 움켜쥐고 어둑한 숲길을 걸으며, 여우는 비로소 깨달았다.

'내가 남에게 한 짓이 고스란히 나에게 돌아왔구나...'

그 뒤로 여우는 다시는 남을 골탕 먹이려 하지 않았다고 한다.

  • 달아오르다: [verb] To grow intensely hot or flush a deep red.
  • 움켜쥐다: [verb] To curl one's fingers and grip something tightly in one's hand.
  • 비로소: [adv.] For the first time; marking the moment when something that had not happened before finally comes about.
  • 고스란히: [adv.] Entirely intact and untouched, exactly as it was before.
  • 골탕(을) 먹이다: [idiom] To play a cruel trick on someone, causing them serious harm or humiliation.

2. The Crane's Repayment

A few days went by. Then the crane came to visit the fox. A gentle smile rested on her face as she spoke.

- Crane: "Master Fox, thank you so very much for dinner the other day. This time I should like to repay your kindness. Would you come to my house tomorrow evening?"

- Fox: "Oh, how splendid! To think I shall get to taste Lady Crane's own cooking — I can hardly wait."

Once again the fox was well-pleased with himself, certain he was about to enjoy another delicious meal at no cost at all.

- Fox: 'After the way I treated her last time, she still invites me over. What a simple-hearted little thing that crane really is.'

The next evening, humming a tune to himself, the fox arrived at the crane's house. From inside drifted the fragrant aroma of fish. The fox gulped down the saliva that rose in his mouth.

- Crane: "Welcome, Master Fox. Tonight I have prepared a dish I made with the greatest care, just for you."

But the moment the fox saw the vessel the crane set before him, his eyes popped wide open. The food had been served in a jar with a long neck and a narrow mouth. The crane slipped her long beak deep inside and began picking out the food with relish. The fox shoved his snout at the opening of the jar, but the mouth was so narrow that not even his nose would fit. He tipped the jar this way and that, he stretched out his tongue as far as it would go, but the very tip of his tongue could barely even graze the food.

- Crane: "Why, you have not touched a thing. Is it perhaps not to your taste?"

The crane asked, feigning concern — and her tone was the very echo of the fox's own words from the other day.

The fox's face flushed a burning red. Everything he had done to the crane the last time had come right back upon him. Unable to utter a single word, he had to head home on an empty stomach. Clutching his hollow belly and walking the dim forest path, the fox at last understood.

- Fox: 'What I did to another has come back to me, every last bit of it...'

And from that day on, it is said, the fox never again tried to play a cruel trick on anyone.
