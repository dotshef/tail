### 1 페이지 (77단어)

어느 숲속 마을에 여우와 두루미가 이웃하여 살고 있었다. 둘은 가끔 인사를 나누는 사이였으나, 깊이 어울린 적은 없었다. 어느 날, 여우가 두루미를 찾아왔다. 여우는 능글맞은 미소를 지으며 말했다.
"두루미 양, 내일 저녁에 우리 집으로 식사하러 오지 않겠소? 내가 특별한 요리를 준비하겠소."
"어머, 정말요? 고맙습니다, 여우 씨. 꼭 가겠어요."
두루미는 기뻐하며 돌아갔다. 그러나 여우는 속으로 킥킥거리며 혼자 웃었다.
"두루미가 저 길다란 부리로 어떻게 먹을지 구경 좀 해 볼까. 재미있겠는걸."
여우는 처음부터 두루미를 놀려 먹을 속셈이었던 것이다.

"왜 안 드시오? 입맛에 안 맞소?"
여우는 걱정하는 척했으나, 입꼬리가 실룩실룩 올라가는 것을 감추지 못했다.
"아니에요, 맛있어요... 다만 제가 조금 배가 아파서요."
두루미는 창피함을 꾹 참으며 대답했다. 결국 두루미는 수프 한 모금 제대로 먹지 못한 채 빈속으로 돌아가야 했다. 여우는 두루미가 돌아간 뒤 혼자 남은 수프까지 깨끗이 핥아 먹으며 배를 잡고 웃었다.
"아이고, 배야! 두루미 꼴 좀 봐라. 저 긴 부리로 쩔쩔매는 게 얼마나 우습던지!"

## 1. The Fox's Mischief

In a small village deep in the forest, Mr. Fox and Miss Crane lived next door to each other as neighbors. Now and then the two would exchange greetings, but they had never truly grown close. One day, Mr. Fox came to call on Miss Crane. With a sly little smile curling at his lips, he spoke.

"Miss Crane, would you care to come over to my house for dinner tomorrow evening? I shall prepare something very special for you."

"Oh my, really? Thank you so much, Mr. Fox. I'll be sure to come."

Miss Crane went home delighted. But Mr. Fox, left alone, giggled quietly to himself.

'Let me see just how she manages to eat with that long beak of hers. This ought to be quite amusing.'

From the very beginning, Mr. Fox had been scheming to make a fool of Miss Crane.

The next evening, Miss Crane set off for Mr. Fox's house with a fluttering heart. Inside his home, the rich, savory scent of soup filled the air, and Miss Crane's mouth began to water.

"Welcome, Miss Crane. Tonight I shall treat you to a soup I've simmered with the greatest care."

Mr. Fox served the soup in a wide, shallow dish. Then, lapping it up eagerly with his broad tongue, he began to eat with relish. One slurp, two slurps — in no time, half the dish was already gone.

Miss Crane lowered her long beak to the dish, but only the very tip could touch the soup, and she couldn't manage a single proper mouthful. No matter how she pecked at it, the soup in the broad, shallow dish only slipped away from her.

"Why aren't you eating? Is it not to your taste?"

Mr. Fox pretended to look concerned, yet he couldn't quite hide the way the corners of his mouth kept twitching upward.

"Oh no, it's delicious... it's only that my stomach is a little upset."

Miss Crane swallowed her embarrassment and answered as best she could. In the end, she had to head home on an empty belly, without so much as a proper sip of soup. Once she was gone, Mr. Fox licked up every last drop of the leftover soup himself, then doubled over clutching his belly with laughter.

"Oh, my sides! Just look at the state of her! How hilarious it was, watching her fumble around with that long beak of hers!"

## 2. The Crane's Return

A few days passed, and Miss Crane came to call on Mr. Fox. With a gentle smile on her face, she spoke.

"Mr. Fox, thank you so very much for the meal the other day. This time, I'd like to return the favor. Would you come over to my house tomorrow evening?"

"Oh, what a lovely idea! I can hardly wait to taste your cooking, Miss Crane."

Mr. Fox was quite pleased with himself, already imagining another delicious meal he could enjoy for free.

'To think she'd invite me even after being made such a fool of last time. Miss Crane really is as simple-hearted as they come.'

The next evening, Mr. Fox arrived at Miss Crane's house humming a cheerful tune. From inside wafted the fragrant aroma of a fish dish, and he gulped down the saliva pooling in his mouth.

"Welcome, Mr. Fox. Tonight I've prepared a dish I made with the greatest care."

When Mr. Fox saw the vessel Miss Crane set before him, his eyes went round with surprise. The dish had been served inside a tall bottle with a long neck and a very narrow opening. Miss Crane slipped her long beak right down into the bottle and happily picked out the food, bite after bite. Mr. Fox shoved his snout against the mouth of the bottle, but the opening was far too narrow — not even his nose would fit inside. He tried tilting the bottle this way and that, he stretched out his tongue as far as it would go, but the very tip of it could barely even brush the food.

"Why aren't you eating? Is it perhaps not to your taste?"

Miss Crane asked, pretending to look concerned. Her tone was almost exactly the same as the one Mr. Fox had used the other day. Mr. Fox's face flushed a deep crimson. Everything he had done to Miss Crane before had come right back to him, just as it was. Unable to say a single word, he had to return home on an empty stomach. Clutching his hollow belly as he walked along the dim forest path, Mr. Fox at last understood.

'Everything I did to another has come back to me, exactly as I gave it...'

From that day on, it is said, Mr. Fox never again tried to play mean tricks on anyone.
